Quantifiers, possessives and demonstratives

## few/little - A2

Quantifiers: a few/a little/a lot/lots

- I have a few books.
- I do a little exercise every day.
- There are a few visitors here already.
- I've only got a little money.

| Countable   | Uncountable    |
| ----------- | -------------- |
| a few books | a little money |

We use a few with countable nouns in positive sentences to talk about a small number of people or things.

- I have a few books.
- There are a few people on the beach.

We use a little with uncountable nouns to talk about a small amount of something.

- I have a little money.
- We have a little time.

We can sometimes use some instead of a few/a little with the same meaning.

- I have some/a few books.
- I do some/a little exercise every day.
