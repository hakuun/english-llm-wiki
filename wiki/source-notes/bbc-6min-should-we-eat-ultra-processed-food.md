## Why it is useful

- 话题非常具体——超加工食品、方便食品——词汇和日常生活直接挂钩，是最容易的 B1-B2 听力之一。
- 包含 4 个表示"偶尔"的表达：`once in a while`, `from time to time`, `now and then`, `occasionally`——B1 词汇丰富度的好例子。
- 短语 `from scratch`（从零开始）和 `guilty pleasure`（罪恶的快感）在日常对话中非常高频。
- 话题与"The future of food"互补——一个讲"未来吃什么"，一个讲"现在吃的东西是不是超加工"。
- 英音清晰，对话节奏正常。

## Key phrases and collocations

- `guilty pleasure` — 罪恶的快感（口语高频）
- `once in a while` / `from time to time` / `now and then` — 偶尔（三选一或交替使用）
- `everything in moderation` — 凡事有度（生活建议中的万能句式）
- `cook from scratch` — 从头开始做饭
- `linked to poor health` — 与健康不良有关（因果关系表达的谨慎措辞）
- `cut (sth) out of (one's) diet` — 从饮食中去掉…

## Comprehension focus

1. What makes a food "ultra-processed"? (Contains 5+ ingredients, including things you wouldn't find in a kitchen — emulsifiers, preservatives, additives, dyes, sweeteners)
2. Why are ultra-processed foods popular? (Convenience — ready meals take a couple of minutes in the microwave vs cooking from scratch)
3. What is the advice about eating ultra-processed foods? (Don't panic — everything is fine in moderation; eat them once in a while but not all the time)
4. When were fizzy drinks invented? (1772 — carbonated water was used to try to prevent scurvy on sea voyages)

## Retelling prompt

1. 什么是超加工食品？举 2-3 个例子。
2. 为什么人们喜欢吃超加工食品？（提到 `convenient`, `ready meal`, `from scratch`）
3. 我们应该完全不吃超加工食品吗？健康建议是什么？（用 `everything in moderation`）
4. (Challenge) What is your guilty pleasure? Describe it in 2-3 simple sentences.

## Difficulty note

这是 8 篇 BBC 材料中最容易的之一——营养和食品类词汇可能与中文概念重叠，不需要额外背景知识。`ultra-processed` 这个词看起来长，但 `ultra-`（极端）+ `processed`（加工的）构词清晰。建议放在听力学习顺序的中间位置（第 4-5 篇），作为从具体话题到抽象话题的过渡。
