### Corrected version

Hello, my name is Haibin Kuang. I am 26 years old. I am a front-end developer, and I have been working since 2020. Now I work for a K-12 education company in Changsha, Hunan Province, China. I want to improve my English to CEFR B2 within one year, and I need authoritative and effective learning materials. Please tell me how to find these materials.

### More natural version

Hello, my name is Haibin Kuang. I am 26 years old and work as a front-end developer at a K-12 education company in Changsha, Hunan Province, China. I have been working in front-end development since 2020. My goal is to improve my English to CEFR B2 within one year. I need authoritative and effective learning materials, so I would like to know how to find them.

### Key learning points

- Capitalize names, places, nationalities, and the first word of a sentence.
- Use `I am 26 years old`, not `my name is ..., 26 years old`.
- Use `I have been working since 2020` for work experience that started in the past and continues now.
- Use `improve my English to CEFR B2`, not `prove my English level to CEFR-B2`.
- Use `within one year`, not `spend a year` in this sentence.
- Use `these materials`, not `this materials`, because `materials` is plural.
