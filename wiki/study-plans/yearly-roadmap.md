# Yearly Roadmap

Goal: reach general CEFR B2 in about one year with about 30 minutes of English study per day.

## Months 1-3: A2 to early B1 foundation

Focus:

- Build high-frequency vocabulary.
- Read short A2-B1 graded texts.
- Listen to slow audio with transcripts.
- Learn practical grammar for sentence accuracy.
- Write simple sentences and short paragraphs.

Avoid:

- Too much difficult authentic material.
- Long grammar theory without use.
- Collecting sources without studying them.

Expected outcome:

- More stable understanding of simple English texts.
- Better sentence accuracy.
- First personal error patterns identified.

## Months 4-8: B1 consolidation to B2 transition

Focus:

- Increase reading length and topic range.
- Add more natural listening with transcripts.
- Learn collocations and word families.
- Write summaries and short opinions.
- Start using some authentic but accessible materials.

Expected outcome:

- Comfortable with many B1 materials.
- Beginning to handle selected B2 materials with support.
- More varied sentence patterns and fewer repeated basic errors.

## Months 9-12: B2 strengthening

Focus:

- Read and listen to B2-level materials more regularly.
- Summarize, compare, explain, and give opinions.
- Improve writing organization and accuracy.
- Review recurring errors until they decrease.
- Use CEFR B2 can-do checks for assessment.

Expected outcome:

- Ability to understand the main ideas of many B2-level materials.
- Ability to write clearer paragraphs on familiar and general topics.
- Stronger self-correction and learning independence.
